What is BMI?

BMI stands for Body Mass Index. It is a measure that uses your weight and height to assess whether you have a healthy body weight. BMI is a useful tool, but it has limitations and does not account for factors like muscle mass and body composition.

Interpreting BMI Results

- BMI less than 18.5: Underweight
- BMI 18.5 to 24.9: Normal weight
- BMI 25 to 29.9: Overweight
- BMI 30 or greater: Obese

Health Risks Associated with BMI

Both low and high BMI levels can be associated with health risks. Underweight individuals may have weakened immune systems, bone loss, and fertility issues. On the other hand, obesity can lead to a higher risk of heart disease, type 2 diabetes, and other chronic conditions.
